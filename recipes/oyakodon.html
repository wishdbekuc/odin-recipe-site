Oyakodon Recipe

back

Super popular both at restaurants and at home, oyakodon (Japanese chicken and egg rice bowl) is sort of like the pizza of Japanâ€”if pizza were the kind of soul-satisfying comfort food that's easy to make at home, with minimal ingredients, in about 20 minutes. This kind of quick and easy one-pot rice bowl is a huge time-saver in the kitchen.

In Japanese, oya means "parent," and ko means "child." Donburi, typically shortened to just don, means "bowl," though, like "paella" or "casserole," it's also the name of any dish served in a donburi. These dishes are frequently (but not always) composed of ingredients simmered together in broth, then poured over rice. In this case, the oya and the ko are chicken and egg.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dashi
  • 2 tbsp sake
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 large onion (thinly sliced)
  • 300g chicken thigh/breast (thinly sliced)
  • scallions
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 ginger slice
  • 2 servings rice

Directions

  1. Combine liquid ingredients together in a pan and simmer over medium heat.
  2. Add sugar and ginger. Stir to dissolve sugar.
  3. Add onions and cook until half-tender, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add in chicken slices and cook until chicken is cooked through. Remove ginger slice.
  5. Beat eggs in a bowl. Add eggs to pan, pouring eggs evenly across the pan.
  6. Leave to cook until desired doneness (slightly runny is good for me!)
  7. Put rice in bowls. Pour the contents of the pan over the top of the rice.
  8. Top with chopped scallions and serve!
